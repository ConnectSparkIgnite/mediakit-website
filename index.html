About

Why Conference in a Box Exists

In August 2025, during the National Prevention Network conference in Washington, D.C., a group of Prevention Leaders gathered informally. Travel budgets were tightening, states and regions were struggling to bring national presenters in, and a long-standing national prevention conference would soon move to a virtual format.

Conference in a Box was created as a win-win solution.

The Problem

Travel budgets are shrinking. Bringing in national-caliber speakers is harder than ever. Piecing together agendas from scratch takes time you don't have.

The Solution

Conference in a Box brings a ready-made, one-day prevention leadership summit directly to your state or region — speakers, agenda, and all.

The Intentional Conference Break

You know that moment? You're sitting in a conference session, the speaker just said something that landed, and you think: "I wish I could sit down and have a cup of coffee with this person."

That's exactly what the Intentional Conference Break is. An extended, unstructured time built into the day where attendees can pull up a chair with the people who just presented — or with other members of the Conference in a Box team. Go deeper into a topic. Talk through a local challenge. Explore a new idea that sparked during a session.

And here's the thing: these aren't your typical stand-at-a-podium presenters. They're facilitators, conversation starters, and practitioners who make sessions interactive and engaging. The ideas they surface don't stay on a slide deck — they follow you into this break and into the work you do next.
